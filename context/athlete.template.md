# Athlete Narrative Context

> This file is your coaching context. It gets injected into every AI call JeZR makes —
> post-workout feedback, weekly reviews, and plan proposals. The richer and more honest
> this is, the more useful JeZR becomes.
>
> Think of it as the conversation you'd have with a new coach on day one — everything
> they'd need to know about you as an athlete to work with you effectively.
>
> Update this file at the start of each training block, after a major race, after an
> injury, or whenever something significant changes. Back it up — this document becomes
> more valuable over time.
>
> Delete this instruction block before saving your personal version.

---

## Background

*Who are you as an athlete? How long have you been training, how did you get into it,
and what does sport mean to you? A few sentences of context helps the AI understand
your relationship with training — whether you're a competitive age-grouper, a
recreational athlete with ambitious goals, or somewhere in between.*

---

## Training history

*What have you done? Key races, milestones, PRs. What training approaches have you
tried — coached, self-directed, structured plans, intuitive? What has worked and what
hasn't? If you've had significant gaps (injury, life, illness) note those too.*

---

## Injury history and risk areas

*Be specific here — this is one of the most important sections. Not just "calf issue"
but: which calf, when, what caused it, how long it took to resolve, what the warning
signs were, and what conditions seem to bring it back. The AI uses this to avoid
repeating patterns that have caused problems before.*

*Example: Left calf, grade 2 strain in March 2025 after a sudden mileage spike.
6 weeks off running. Tends to tighten up when weekly volume increases more than ~15%
in a single week, or after back-to-back hard days. Right lower back also flares
occasionally — seems correlated with long sitting periods during travel weeks.*

---

## What a good week looks like

*Describe your ideal training week in plain language — not just the sessions but the
feel of it. What's the balance between hard and easy? How do you like your long run
structured? What does recovery look like for you? How do you know when a week has
gone well?*

---

## What a hard week looks like

*What derails a training week for you? Travel, work stress, poor sleep, illness?
How do you typically respond — push through, back off, something in between?
The AI needs to know your tendencies so it can plan around them, not against them.*

---

## How you respond to load

*Some athletes absorb high volume easily but crack under intensity. Others handle
hard sessions fine but need more recovery days. Some do well with consistency and
fall apart with variation. Where do you sit? What signals tell you you're carrying
too much fatigue — heavy legs, elevated resting HR, poor sleep, mood?*

---

## Riding and cross-training

*If you ride — what does that look like? Zwift, outdoor, race bike, trainer? Do you
treat rides as recovery or as training stimulus? Are there specific ride formats that
leave you cooked for days (long climbs, Zwift races, hard intervals)? How do you
balance riding and running load in a typical week?*

*Note: A hard Zwift effort can significantly impact run quality for 1-3 days even if
training load numbers look moderate. Be specific about rides that hit you hard.*

---

## Racing and key sessions

*How do you approach race day — conservative and build, go out at goal pace, or
something else? What's your history with pacing? Do you tend to go out too hard?
What race conditions affect you most (heat, hills, wind)?*

*For key sessions — how do you approach hard workouts? Do you tend to go harder than
prescribed, or hold back? Do you do better with specific targets or general guidance?*

---

## What good coaching feedback looks like to you

*What kind of feedback helps you? Direct and blunt, or more considered? Do you want
the AI to flag when you've underperformed against a plan, or focus on what went well?
How do you handle hearing that a session wasn't executed well?*

*Example: Direct is fine — I'd rather know if a session was off-target than have it
glossed over. Focus on what the data says, not on encouragement.*

---

## Life context

*What does your life look like around training? Work schedule, travel demands, family
commitments, sleep patterns. This context helps the AI plan realistic weeks rather
than ideal ones. A plan that looks perfect on paper but ignores that you travel every
second week will consistently fail.*

---

## Fuelling and nutrition

*What has worked and what hasn't in training and racing? Any GI issues, things to
avoid, protocols that have proven reliable? The more specific the better — generic
fuelling advice is not useful here.*

---

## Notes and observations

*Anything else worth capturing — patterns you've noticed, things you're working on,
current experiments, how you're feeling about training right now. This section can be
more informal and conversational.*

---

## Race reports and key session reflections

*Add entries here after significant races or key sessions. Date, what happened, what
you learned. Over time this becomes the most valuable part of the document — a record
of what actually works for you under race conditions.*

*Format: Date — Event — What happened — Key takeaway*
